Jaws Opening Credits Reimagined

As part of a university project, I reinterpreted the opening credits of Jaws by integrating my artistic vision. While respecting the tense atmosphere and underlying threat of the film, I explored visual and sound contrasts to create a personal graphic universe.

Brief

Recreate the opening credits of the film "Jaws" by Steven Spielberg in your own way. This is not about copying the original opening credits, but rather creating your own interpretation. You can, however, give a nod to the original if you wish. Use this exercise to express yourself as you wish. Typography games, hand-drawn illustrations, vector illustrations, geometric shapes... do what you love to do!

Expected Graphic Style

Free graphic style but respecting the atmosphere of the film (Thriller, suspense, marine...)

Constraints

Duration should be approximately 30 seconds You must have all the given text appear. You must use Photoshop or Illustrator to prepare your mockups. Sound design is expected to emphasize certain movements.

Original Opening Credits

From the opening, Jaws plunges us into dark and unsettling water, suggesting invisible danger. Spielberg chooses not to show the shark, strengthening the viewer's imagination, while John Williams' minimalist music—two repeated low notes—establishes memorable and chilling tension.

Post-production

Moodboard

This moodboard, inspired by Jaws, clearly shows the atmosphere of the film with lots of blue, black and red. It features both the mysterious beauty of the ocean and the danger hidden beneath. The images transition from calm sea scenes to more turbulent moments, with red flashes reminiscent of blood and attacks. This moodboard also explores different graphic styles for the motion design realization.
